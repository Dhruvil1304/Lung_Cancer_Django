Lung Cancer Precautions

Here are some facts about lung cancer: Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer deaths in the U.S. Survival rates are low, but they are improving The average five-year survival rate for all lung cancer patients is 18.6 percent Patients diagnosed with lung cancer at an early stage via CT screening have a 20-year survival rate of 80 percent Lung cancer life expectancy can vary by the type of lung cancer and other factors In general, life expectancy for lung cancer patients ranges from 7-16 months Lung cancer is usually considered curable if caught in the early stages It's usually not curable if it spreads to distant tissues People who have never smoked account for 20% of lung cancer diagnoses every year An estimated 47,660 non-smokers will be diagnosed with lung cancer in 2023 Other facts about lung cancer include: Racial disparities exist in lung cancer Lung cancer screening and research has the potential to save thousands of lives With advances in treatment, more people are living longer and living well with lung cancer
